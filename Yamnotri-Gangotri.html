Yamunotri

Yamunotri is located in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand, India, at an altitude of 3,293 meters (10,804 feet) above sea level. It is the source of the Yamuna River, one of the most sacred rivers in India. Yamunotri is dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, the sister of Yama (the god of death) and the daughter of Surya (the Sun god). The temple was originally built by Maharani Guleria of Jaipur in the 19th century. According to Hindu mythology, bathing in the waters of Yamuna is believed to cleanse all sins and protect from an untimely death.

Main Attractions::-

Yamunotri Temple: The primary place of worship, where the idol of Goddess Yamuna is enshrined. Surya Kund: A hot water spring near the temple, where pilgrims cook rice and potatoes as prasad (sacred offering). Divya Shila: A rock pillar near the Surya Kund, worshipped before entering the temple. Yamunotri Glacier: The actual source of the Yamuna River, located at an altitude of 4,421 meters (14,505 feet).

Gangotri Dham:-

Gangotri is located in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand, India, at an altitude of 3,100 meters (10,200 feet) above sea level. It is the origin of the Ganges River, known as Bhagirathi at its source. Gangotri is dedicated to Goddess Ganga, the personification of the Ganges River. The temple was built by the Gorkha General Amar Singh Thapa in the early 18th century. According to Hindu mythology, King Bhagirath performed penance here to bring the River Ganga to earth to cleanse the ashes of his ancestors.

Main Attractions::-

Gangotri Temple: The main shrine dedicated to Goddess Ganga. Bhairon Ghati: A scenic spot 10 km downstream from Gangotri, where the Bhagirathi and Jadh Ganga rivers meet. Gaumukh Glacier: The actual source of the Bhagirathi River, located about 19 km from Gangotri. Pandava Gufa: A cave where the Pandavas are believed to have meditated during their journey to the Himalayas.
